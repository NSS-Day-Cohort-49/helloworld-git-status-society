Greece

Greece is a beautiful country with ancient roots. It was the birthplace of many foundations of Western culture, and every city has its history preserved in many ancient sites. Greece has breathtaking beaches, turquoise waters, green lakes, and rugged mountains - everywhere you look, there is something beautiful to take in. Below are three famous cities that everyone should visit on their vacation to Greece.

Santorini, Greece

Athens

Athens is steeped in history and culture, from the ancient ruins dotted around the city, to its fascinating museums. While you're here, you should visit the Acropolis, an ancient citadel that holds some of the most famous historical cites in the country. Its thousands of years old and home to incredible ruins, including the world-famous Parthenon temple and Theatre of Dionysus.

The Parthenon in Athens
Thessaloniki

Thessaloniki is home to fine food, rich history, stunning coastlines, and so much more. Vibrant and full of things to do, this Greek city houses Byzantine landmarks scattered through its ancient, yet modern terrain. The White Tower, shown above, has a history of operating as a prison and a place of execution. Apart from history, this attracts more people due to its massive nature and is also located in a prominent position.

The White Tower
Delphi

A mystical place where mythology and history collide, the ancient sanctuary of Delphi often leaves the visitor breathless. As the most important oracle in ancient Greece, it welcomed kings and mortals, coming from across the ancient world to hear from the oracle of god Apollo. This temple is where the oracle was housed and where Apollo was worshipped.
